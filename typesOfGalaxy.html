Types Of Galaxies

        We currently know three main types of galaxies, with a few subtypes. Here’s a brief overview:

Spiral Galaxies

Shape: Flat, rotating disk with spiral arms.
Stars: Mix of young and old stars.
Example: Milky Way, Andromeda Galaxy.
Fun fact: Spiral arms are sites of active star formation.

Elliptical Galaxies

Shape: Rounded or oval, smooth appearance.
Stars: Mostly older stars, very little gas or dust.
Example: M87 in Virgo Cluster.
Fun fact: Often found in galaxy clusters as “giant ellipticals.”

Irregular Galaxies

Shape: No definite structure, chaotic appearance.
Fun fact: Often result from galaxy collisions or gravitational interactions.
Stars: Mix of young stars, lots of gas and dust.
Example: Large Magellanic Cloud.
