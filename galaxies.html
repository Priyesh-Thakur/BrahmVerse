A galaxy is a massive system of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter held together by gravity. They come in three main types: spiral (like our Milky Way), elliptical (round or oval), and irregular (no clear shape). Galaxies can contain millions to trillions of stars and are often grouped into clusters and superclusters.
