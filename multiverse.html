MultiVerse

As of now, we only have direct knowledge of one universe—our own observable universe. Everything we can measure, observe, and study scientifically is within this universe.

The concept of multiple universes or a multiverse exists in theoretical physics, but these are mostly speculative ideas based on mathematics and theory, not observation. Some key points:

Observable Universe – This is the part of the universe we can see, limited by the speed of light and the age of the universe (~13.8 billion years).

Beyond Observable – There may be more universe beyond what we can see, but we have no data about it yet.

Multiverse Theories – Hypothetical ideas suggest there could be many universes, e.g.,:

Bubble universes (from cosmic inflation)

Many-worlds interpretation (from quantum mechanics)

Parallel dimensions in string theory

But so far, only one universe has empirical evidence. All others are purely theoretical.
